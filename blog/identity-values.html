A snapshot of my ethics & Values...

Having worked public libraries I have had a number of ethical dilemmas to deal with. As a PSA representative, my manager brought to light an example of a coworker(1) complaining privately via email to her on anothers(2) behalf against a third party member(3) of alleged bullying. Worker 2 came to me stressed out about the complaint fearing for the outcome and descendiing into an anxiety attack. Working with 3, I knew that my manager was taking care of that issue and working through things with 3 and I didn't want to explain the situation of what 1 did to 2 as that would have made things quite messy, so when asked by 1 what was happening with 2, I mentioned that she was upset over a professional issue. 1 knew I was a PSA representative and went into damage control and went to help 1 out of her stress. I weighed up the confidentiality of the situation against what could allow the parties affected to minimise their stress and realise the consequences of their own actions at the same time. I'm still unsure if my methods were ideal...

My background...

I come from a bit of a mixed maori and pakeha background... My parents came from big strict families and I was brought up with those pakeha values as well. It shaped me in weird ways, where I know right and wrong and try to balance my own moral compass against that and what every situation brings. I am quite libaral minded, but as a I get older (and where I have lived), many of those views get challenged (even within myself).
