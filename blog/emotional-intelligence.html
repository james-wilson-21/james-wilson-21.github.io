What is Emotional Intelligence?

"the capacity to be aware of, control, and express one's emotions, and to handle interpersonal relationships judiciously and empathetically"

"emotional intelligence is the key to both personal and professional success"

Along with one's emotions, the ability to monitor other people's emotions as well as one's own and recognise those emotions is important too. Once that is acheived it can guide your own behaviour. This can be broken down into ablilities: percieving, using, understanding, and managing emotions.

How is it different to IQ?

IQ traditionally is a number derived from a standardized test. The IQ test used to measure this takes into account a variety of things from: Visual and spatial processing, Knowledge of the world, Fluid reasoning, Working memory and short-term memory, Quantitative reasoning

Why is it important?

Emotional intelligence helps with a number of important parts of our day to day lives including: resilieence, motivation, empathy, resoning, stress and communication and more. By learning and growing emotional intelligence you can live a more fulfilled life. This can be achieved through, more successful and meaningful interactions and working more cohesively with others.
