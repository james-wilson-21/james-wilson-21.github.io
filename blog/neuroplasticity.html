Neuroplasticity & the Growth Mindset

How Understanding the Principles of neuroplasticity benefits people

The change and growth of the neural networks in the brain by either making new connections (or pathways) or creating new ones is a key factor to know for people wishing to work on or learn a new skill in whatever field they desire. -People are able then to learn and achieve new things through practice to keep neuralpathways open. -Improve exiesting pathways -The value of repitition in training those pathways -the value of intense work and practice in working on a skill -taking the time to work on new and existing skills and understanding the time between sessions -Knowing which parts of the skill acquisition is important and not overloading with too much input

engaging with the principles of neuroplasticity

Repitition is important and setting a time and period of frequency to work on a new or existing skill will aid me in being able to improve my skills in Web and software development. It will also aid me in focussing on those skills which I can recognise are of greater value for what I want to achieve instead of overloading myself and trying to info dump my brain to less effect.

What is a growth mindset?

Growth mindset is the engagement of someone who believes that with a desire to get smarter they can recognise that effort and time they put in will lead to achievment. The opposite of this is the fixed mindset, where a person believes that there skills and intelligence are fixed with no room left to grow. A growth mindset allows people to learn new skills and improve in areas they set there mind to. This can be influenced both within and without through environmental factors. Similarly the fixed mindset can be affected, for example, in telling a child that they are intelligent or smart, can encourge a fixed mindset.
